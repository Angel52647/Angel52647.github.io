Greivis Vásquez

An icon of Venezuelan basketball

🧒 Early Years

Greivis Josué Vásquez Rodríguez was born on January 16, 1987, in Caracas, Venezuela. From a young age, he displayed a passion for basketball, excelling in his home country before moving to the United States to continue his athletic development.

🎓 College Career

Vásquez attended Montrose Christian School in Maryland, where he played alongside future NBA stars, including Kevin Durant. He later joined the University of Maryland, where he excelled as a point guard and was awarded the Bob Cousy Award for Outstanding College Point Guard in 2010.

🏀 Professional NBA Career

In 2010, he was selected by the Memphis Grizzlies in the first round of the NBA Draft. Throughout his career, he played for several teams, including the New Orleans Hornets, Sacramento Kings, Toronto Raptors, Milwaukee Bucks, and Brooklyn Nets. Despite injuries, Vásquez left an indelible mark on the league.

🌍 International Legacy

In addition to his success in the NBA, Vásquez was a mainstay of the Venezuelan national team, representing his country in multiple international competitions and helping raise the profile of Venezuelan basketball on the world stage.

📺 New Professional Stage

After retiring from the court, Vásquez has ventured into the media, working as a commentator for the Washington Wizards, sharing his experience and knowledge of basketball with fans.
